One proposal for taking people to Mars involves a \quotation{space bus} in a permanent orbit that passes close to the orbits of both Earth and Mars.

\startalignment[center]
\dontleavehmode
\externalfigure[TestFigBus][width=6cm]
\stopalignment

What would the period of the bus's orbit about the sun be?  (Ignore the gravitational force that the planets exert on the bus.)

{\bf Useful Information:} You can assume the orbits are centered circles. The radius of the Earth's orbit is $1.50\sci{11}\units{m}$.  The radius of the Mars's orbit is $2.28\sci{11}\units{m}$.  One year is $3.16\sci{7}\units{s}$.
